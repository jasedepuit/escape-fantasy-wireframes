Screen 27
Team Leaderboard
The leaderboard view for team-based competitions. Replaces Screen 8 (individual leaderboard) when viewing a team-format competition. Each row represents a team, not an individual player.
Header & Tabs
1
Competition name in header — shows the team competition name (e.g. "EC World Series 2026"). Back arrow returns to Home or Competition Browser depending on entry point.
2
Search icon — find a specific team by name. Useful when there are many entries and the user wants to locate a friend's team.
3
Leaderboard | Leagues tabs — Leaderboard shows all teams globally. Leagues tab filters to teams within a specific league, consistent with Screen 8 behaviour.
My Team Stats
4
Team name displayed prominently — the user's team name appears in orange alongside the "My Team" label, so it's immediately clear whose stats are shown. This replaces the individual "My Stats" card from Screen 8.
5
Rank / Points / Pts Behind — same three stats as the individual leaderboard, but scoped to the team's performance across all races in the competition. Tapping Rank scrolls the list to the user's team row and triggers a pulse highlight.
Team Rows
6
Team name + manager name — each row shows the team name (bold) and the manager's display name below it in muted text, so users can associate teams with the people they know.
7
Position change badge — same green/red up/down badge as Screen 8, reflecting movement since the last race result was processed.
8
Top 3 rank highlight — positions 1–3 show the rank in orange, consistent with Screen 8.
9
Tap any row — navigates to the Team Overview screen (Screen 20) for that team, showing their roster and per-rider points breakdown. Read-only for other teams.
10
My team row — highlighted with an orange tint background and bold team name/points. Tapping navigates to the user's own Team Overview (editable if transfer window is open).
This screen is the team-format equivalent of Screen 8. The routing logic should detect competition format and serve either Screen 8 or Screen 27 accordingly — they are never shown simultaneously for the same competition.
