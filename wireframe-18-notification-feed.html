Screen 18 · Notification Feed — Annotations
① Entry Point
Accessed by tapping the bell icon on the Home screen only. The bottom nav Home tab stays active to make clear this is a child of Home, not a separate tab. The Home tab badge dot signals unread notifications wherever the user is in the app — the most natural and expected pattern on iOS/Android.
② Preferences Shortcut
The ⚙ icon in the header navigates to Notification Preferences (Screen 17). Placing it here means users who want to reduce noise can reach settings directly from the context where they're experiencing that noise — more intuitive than navigating Account → Notification Preferences.
③ Mark All as Read
Clears all unread states in one tap. Appears only when at least one notification is unread. Individual rows are also marked read on tap. No delete/dismiss — notifications persist for a rolling window (e.g. 30 days) and are not user-deletable. If the list becomes very long, consider pagination or a "load earlier" pattern rather than infinite scroll.
④ Picks Lock Warning
Time-sensitive notifications show at the top regardless of strict chronological order. Tapping navigates directly to My Picks for that stage — one tap from notification to action. The lock warning is the highest-priority notification type in the app; consider a more urgent visual treatment (e.g. accent border) if multiple lock warnings stack.
⑤ Rank Change
Positive rank changes (↑) shown in green, negative (↓) in red. Tapping navigates to the relevant leaderboard — either the competition leaderboard or the league leaderboard depending on context. Both rank-up and rank-down are shown; rank-down notifications are off by default (configurable in Notification Preferences).
⑥ League Enrolled Notification
Generated when a league director enrolls your league into a new competition. Tapping navigates to the Competition detail for that race, so the member can review and set their picks. This is the in-app complement to the push notification for the same event.
Home Tab Badge Dot
The orange dot on the Home nav tab appears whenever there are unread notifications. It disappears once the user visits this screen and all notifications are marked read (either manually or via "Mark all read"). This is the standard iOS/Android convention — the badge lives on the tab that owns the content, so users know exactly where to tap.
