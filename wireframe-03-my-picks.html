①
Competition Switcher
Shows the current competition context on every Compete sub-screen. Tapping "Change ›" returns to the Competition Browser without resetting state.
②
Stats Bar — Re-pick & Auto Pick
Two race-level controls. Left: Re-pick indicator in green when available (turns grey once used — allows changing one past pick if the rider hasn't scored yet). Right: Auto Pick — fills all unpicked stages using the AI engine. Positioned here, not on the Today card, to make clear this is a whole-race action.
③
Cut-off Strip
Only shown on race days when a pick window is open. Pulsing dot + countdown draws attention to the deadline. Disappears once the stage starts. If no stage is live today, this strip is hidden entirely.
④
Today's Stage Card
The only element that breaks out of the list pattern. Orange border signals this is the current action item. Shows current pick with avatar initials, cut-off time, and a clear Update pick CTA. Tapping "Update pick →" navigates to Rider Selection (Screen 4) pre-filtered to today's stage. "Get AI suggestion" is a per-stage recommendation, distinct from the race-level Auto Pick.
⑤
Completed Stage Rows
Single line: stage number · rider name · points. Colour indicates performance: green = top 5 finish, amber = top 10, grey = outside top 10. "auto" tag on Stage 3 shows the system made that pick (user missed the cut-off) — no penalty counter, just a label for transparency.
⑥
Future Stage — No Pick → Rider Selection
Italic "No pick yet" with a small "Pick →" button. Date shown when more than 24h away. Tapping anywhere on the row navigates to the Rider Selection screen (Screen 4) filtered to that stage — not a popup or inline expand. This applies to all future stage rows regardless of state (no pick, pre-picked, or injury alert).
⑦
Injury Alert — Stage 17
A small red dot next to the date signals a rider concern. The Pick button turns red and changes to "Change →" to make the action clear. No modal or overlay — keeps the list clean. A push notification was also sent.
