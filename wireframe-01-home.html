Interaction Annotations
1
My Competitions — section behaviour
Shows all competitions the user is enrolled in, ordered by urgency (tightest cut-off first). Cards stack vertically and scroll. Tapping anywhere on a card body navigates to My Picks for that competition. If the user has no active competitions, this section shows an empty state card: "No active competitions — browse to join one" with a CTA to the Compete tab.
2
Status badges — Active / Joined / Finished
Active (green) = live, picking open. Joined (orange) = enrolled, not yet started. Finished (grey) = competition complete. The format badge (Stage Race / Team Competition) is always shown alongside status. Users should never need to open a competition to know whether it is live.
3
"X of Y players" — total participant count
Directly resolves the #1 survey complaint: "I'm 214th — but out of how many?" The total is always shown alongside rank. The ▲2 indicator shows rank movement since the previous stage result — colour-coded green (gain) or red (drop). Tap the rank/points area to go to the full leaderboard.
4
Today's pick — two states
Pick made: shows rider surname-first (per PRD), team, and rider type badge. No pick yet: shows the dashed-circle slot with an orange "Pick your rider for Stage N ›" prompt. The entire pick area is tappable and goes to Rider Selection. Rider names are displayed surname, firstname throughout the app.
5
Pick cut-off countdown — urgency states
Three states: >4h: subdued grey text. <4h: amber. <2h: orange with pulsing dot (as shown). <1h: red. Time displayed in the user's local timezone — not race timezone. Resolves the AU/US pain point of European race cut-offs. Implementation depends on Open Question #6.
6
"Update Pick" / "Pick Rider" CTA — deep-link shortcut
Tapping this button deep-links directly to the Rider Selection screen for that specific stage — skipping the My Picks list. This is the primary engagement path from the Home screen. The button is disabled and visually greyed out once the cut-off has passed. After a stage is locked, it is replaced by the result indicator (points earned).
7
Upcoming competitions — join flow
Shows competitions open to join but not yet started. "Join" taps to the Competition Browser at that competition's detail. Once joined, the button changes to Joined ✓ (non-tappable). This section collapses if no upcoming competitions exist. Maximum two upcoming competitions shown here before a "View all →" link appears.
8
News feed — editorial integration
Pulls from escapecollective.com CMS. Stage Preview articles are pinned at top during an active stage window; other content in reverse-chronological order below. Tapping opens the in-app article reader — no browser redirect. Articles tagged to the user's active competitions are prioritised. Depends on Open Question #10 (CMS content tagging implementation).
Open Questions (Section 9) Affecting This Screen
Q4 — Spoiler protection default: Should the competition card show a completed stage's result (rider's finishing position + points earned) automatically when the user opens the app? This wireframe shows results hidden by default. If the team decides results should display by default with an opt-out, the card needs a "results hidden · tap to reveal" state with a toggle. Recommend: hidden by default for new users (per PRD Section 4.7.2).
Q6 — Timezone handling: The cut-off countdown (annotation 5) must show in the user's local timezone. Two implementation options: (a) user manually sets timezone in Account settings, or (b) auto-detect from device locale. The wireframe assumes option (b), but this needs confirming before dev. Especially critical for AU/US users picking for European morning races.
